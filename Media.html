DOCUMENTARIES

Traceable (2014)

Traceable follows emerging Fashion Designer Laura Siegel and the journey to develop her Fall/Winter 2013 collection using ethical and transparent practices. It sets against the backdrop of the fast fashion industry and our increasing disconnect of where and how clothing is made, and the hands that create a garment.

Writer and director:
Jennifer K. Sharpe

The True Cost (2015)

This is a story about the clothes we wear, the people who make them, and the impact the industry is having on the world. The price of clothing has been decreasing, while the human and environmental costs have grown dramatically. The True Cost is an unequalled project that invites us on an eye opening journey.

Writer and director:
Andrew Morgan

Sweatshop: Deadly Fashion (2015)

Three fashion bloggers who have never given much thought to the people who make their clothes are now trading their comfortable lives for those of Cambodian garment workers. Working in the factories and surviving on $3 a day, this documentary shows the consequences of cheap fashion.

Director: Joakim Kleven
Production: Hacienda Films A.S., APTV

RiverBlue (2016)

Producer and director: Roger Williams

Clothing Poverty: The Hidden World Of Fast Fashion And Second-Hand Clothes (2015)

"You look good in those jeans. But are those jeans themselves good?"
Clothing Poverty takes readers on a global journey, from fabric to fashion show, to reveal the worldwide commodity chains and hidden trade networks that transect the globe and perpetuate poverty.

Author: Andrew Brooks

Fashionopolis:
The Price Of
Fast Fashion
And The Future
Of Clothes (2019)

The clothing industry's exploitation of fellow humans, that the author consider as the fashion bulimia's victims, and the environment has reached epic levels. Fashionopolis details the damage wrought by fashion's behemoths and celebrates the visionaries fighting for change.

Author: Dana Thomas

Overdressed: The Shockingly High Cost Of Cheap Fashion (2012)

98% of our clothing is now made abroad. It is driving up our pace of consumption and turning fashion into one of the most environmentally destructive industries on Earth. This book uncovers the true cost of cheap fashion. What are we doing with all these clothes and what are they doing to us, our planet, even our souls?

Author: Elizabeth Cline

The Conscious Closet: The Revolutionary Guide to Looking Good While Doing Good (2012)

A call to action to transform the very polluting fashion industry into a force for good. Readers will learn where and how their clothes are made and how to start to love and understand their clothes again - without sacrificing the environment, their morals, or style.

Author: Elizabeth Cline
