Fashion Nova and the Instagram era 

"Fashion Nova makes fast fashion for the Instagram elite.
The way many of its garments are made is much less glamorous."


Fashion Nova has perfected fast fashion for the Instagram era. The online retailer leans on a vast network of celebrities, influencers, and random selfie-takers who post about the brand relentlessly on social media. “They need to buy a lot of different styles and probably only wear them a couple of times so their Instagram feeds can stay fresh,” Richard Saghian, Fashion Nova’s founder, said in an interview. To enable that habit, Fashion Nova gives consumers a constant stream of new options (between 600 and 900 new styles each week) that are (very low) priced to sell.

Thanks to social media, the brand has developed a symbiotic relationship with popular influencers, like Cardi B and Cassie, who can turn whatever they wear into an instant trend, feeding shoppers' desire to buy more.


Cassie, a Fashion Nova's ambassador, in a photoshoot for the brand.

Fast fashion has democratized luxury trends for everyday shoppers (who now have the option to dress like their favorite influencers), but it comes at a cost not reflected in its price tag. The New York Times published a report on Fashion Nova, revealing that factories that were making Fashion Nova garments were under investigation by the US Labor Department for underpaying workers and owing them millions in back wages.

That revelation is hardly surprising, given how the brand releases hundreds of styles a week at very low prices. Fashion Nova — and the collective fast fashion ecosystem — was condemned and criticized online, but the report seemed to create no significant shockwaves. Celebrities, influencers, and high-profile “Nova ambassadors”, who helped build the brand’s reputation, still endorse it, and people continue to shop from the brand.
