Large And Small Scale Solutions

What can be done?

"The greatest threat to our planet is the belief
that someone else will save it."


With the fast fashion industry being large and complex, there is not one direct solution, but one thing is for sure: it is society who makes solutions reality.

Large scale solutions
On a bigger scale, new textile innovations can help reduce toxic waste during production, decrease microplastic release and make them more biodegradable. These new innovations help promote a circular model of fast fashion.

One example of a textile innovation are recycling programs, which help divert old textiles and waste from landfills to create new products. A Swedish company called Re:newcell turns old cellulose fibres into pulp, that is used to make new cellulose fibres. Even polyester is easily recyclable, and can be made out of old PET plastic such as plastic bottles. (Cleantech group 2019)

Recycled polyester gives a second life to a material that is not biodegradable
and would otherwise end up in landfill or the ocean.
Source: Fashion United

Another step towards a more sustainable fashion is "take-back" programs. Companies such as H&M and Patagonia offer programs where you can give back your unwanted garments to be recycled into new fibres, although some criticize these programs as they could encourage more consumerism. (Independent 2018)

The brand Reformation has become a leading name in the sustainable fashion world, as they are extremely transparent with their supply chain, use sustainable and deadstock fabric (extra fabric leftover from production) and fund projects to offset their carbon emissions. (Reformation 2018)

Patagonia's 'Don't buy this jacket' campaign promoting comsumption reduction.
Source: Bonfire

Small scale solutions
Regarding consumer's behaviour, small actions that can be done in everyday life to promote sustainable fashion.

Choosing to consume less and better is one simple step to change the way the industry works. Furthermore, choosing to support companies that produce clothing in a way that is less harmful to the environment and the people making the clothes, it sends a message to fast fashion companies about what people really want.

Fast fashion brands are also starting to become more transparent with their supply chain process, allowing consumers to make informed decisions. Earlier this year, H&M announced it would release all of its supply chain information detailing production country, supplier names, factory names, addresses, number of workers working in the factory, and the specific garments used to make the products. (Refinery 29 2019)


“By being transparent about where our products are made, we hope to encourage customers to be more conscious.”
- Isak Roth, H&M’s head of sustainability
Source: Retail Wire


The issue of fast fashion stems from the increased demand for cheaper clothing. If consumers switch to purchasing higher quality items (that last longer) less often, this could seriously change the industry.

Other options include choosing to shop second hand, extending the lives of these already worn and purchased items. Even with clothes that you have already purchased, choosing to recycle, donate, or upcycle clothes has a positive impact on the life cycle of the item. Extending the life of an item or creating a closed-loop system for clothes will help decrease the number of textiles that end up in the landfill.

These small but impactful changes will help move the fast fashion industry towards a more sustainable future.

Source: Esri
