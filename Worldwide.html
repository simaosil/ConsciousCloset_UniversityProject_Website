Santiago, 48 | Los Angeles

"I’m always trying to figure out how to save money, how to buy food, how to not eat out too much." Santiago has worked in many factories. They are often windowless and dirty, with little ventilation. When he first moved to Los Angeles, Santiago was working 11-hour shifts, every day. Now he works about 50 hours a week, earning up to $350.

Maria Silva, 46 | Caruaru, Brazil

Ms. Silva's last job was stitching side seams together in an assembly line with an hourly quota. “Some companies, like the one I worked for, no longer have employees inside the factory and the seamstresses work from home,” she said. “They establish small groups and they are paid per item, so they have the same production without any costs.”

Catherine Gamet, 48 | France

Ms. Gamet began working with leather when she was 16 years old and has been employed by Vuitton for 23 years. "It is my passion,” she said. “That’s how I got into it.” About 800 employees work in Saint-Pourçain. Ms. Gamet said the workshops are well organized, bright and modern. “The time flies by,” she said.

Rukhsana, 48 | Pakistan

"It’s impossible to survive on the salary the textile mills pay. Are we supposed to choose between buying food or paying for clothes and medicine?" Rukhsana works 7 days a week. “The hardest thing about working in a textile mill is if anyone calls you from home — with good or bad news — you can’t take the call and management doesn’t tell you until the day is over."

S, 33 | India

"We don’t even have the freedom to drink water." S.’s shift begins at 9 a.m. She feels a lot of pressure from supervisors to reach quotas of about 90 to 120 pieces per hour. Employees who can’t keep up are often pulled aside at the end of each hour, she said, and supervisors will yell at them and bang on tables.

Seak Hong, 36 | Cambodia

"I feel tired, but I have no choice. I have to work." Ms. Hong wakes up at 4:35 a.m. to catch the truck to work. Her workday begins at 7 and usually lasts nine hours, with a lunch break. Ms. Hong has been in the garment business for 22 years. She earns about $230 a month and supports her father, her brother (who is on disability) and her son.
